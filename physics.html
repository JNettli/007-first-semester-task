Sir Isaac Newton
The Father of Physics
A brilliant English physicist and mathematician, is a prominent figure in the history of science. His unparalleled contributions have shaped our understanding of the natural world. Newton's laws of motion and universal gravitation are cornerstones of modern physics.

Newton's Laws of Motion are fundamental principles in physics that describe the motion of

1. First Law - Law of Inertia: An object at rest will stay at rest, and an object in motion will stay in motion with the same speed and direction, unless acted upon by an external force.

2. Second Law - Law of Acceleration: The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force applied to it and inversely proportional to its mass. This can be expressed as the equation F = ma, where F is the force applied, m is the mass of the object, and a is the resulting acceleration.

3. Third Law - Law of Action and Reaction: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. This means that whenever an object exerts a force on another object, the second object exerts an equal and opposite force on the first.

These laws provide a framework for understanding the behavior of objects in motion and have applications in various fields, including engineering, mechanics, and astrophysics.
